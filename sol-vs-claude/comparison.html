CLAUDE’s most useful preservation

It keeps the reasons behind TS-versus-TSX parsing, shallow-versus-deep class scanning, cyclic parent links, and computed property handling close to the code.

FABLE as a complementary reference

FABLE lands one line shorter than CLAUDE and retains nearly the same amount of commentary. Its main difference is editorial: it keeps physical-line suppression semantics and the shallow/deep split explicit, while dropping examples around JSX names and computed properties.
